1. Introduction

In the digital age, communication is a cornerstone of effective learning and collaboration within academic institutions. While public messaging platforms like WhatsApp, Telegram, and Messenger have revolutionized how people connect, they are not tailored for the unique needs of educational environments. These platforms often introduce distractions, privacy concerns, and non-academic interruptions, making them less suitable for focused academic discussions and departmental coordination.

Late Babanrao Deshmukh College, Camp, Amravati, recognized the need for a dedicated communication platform that addresses these challenges. BDC Messenger was conceptualized as a solution to provide students and faculty with a secure, distraction-free, and college-specific environment for communication. The platform is designed to facilitate academic discussions, departmental announcements, peer-to-peer collaboration, and administrative coordination, all within a controlled and private ecosystem.

BDC Messenger leverages modern web technologies to deliver real-time messaging, group chat management, and robust authentication features. Its responsive design ensures accessibility across devices, empowering users to stay connected and informed whether on campus or remote. By focusing on the specific requirements of the college community, BDC Messenger aims to enhance productivity, foster collaboration, and uphold privacy standards that are often lacking in public messaging apps.

2. Aim and Objectives

Aim

The primary aim of BDC Messenger is to create a dedicated, secure, and distraction-free communication platform exclusively for the students and faculty of Late Babanrao Deshmukh College, Camp, Amravati. Unlike public messaging apps such as WhatsApp, Telegram, or Messenger—where distractions, privacy concerns, and non-academic interruptions are common—BDC Messenger focuses on academic discussions, departmental coordination, announcements, and peer communication in a controlled, college-specific environment.

Objectives

  • Enable secure, real-time messaging between students and faculty.
  • Support group chats with advanced admin controls, including member management and group information updates.
  • Provide robust user authentication and password management for privacy and security.
  • Facilitate academic discussions, departmental announcements, and peer collaboration.
  • Ensure a mobile-friendly and responsive user interface for accessibility on all devices.
  • Minimize distractions and non-academic interruptions by restricting access to college members only.
  • Showcase advanced features suitable for college-level academic submission and future scalability.

3. Literature Review

The evolution of digital communication has significantly impacted educational institutions, enabling faster information exchange and collaborative learning. However, the use of public messaging platforms such as WhatsApp, Telegram, and Messenger in academic settings presents several challenges, including privacy concerns, distractions, and lack of institutional control.

Existing Messaging Platforms: Public messaging apps are designed for general use and lack features tailored for academic environments. Studies have shown that while these platforms facilitate communication, they often lead to non-academic interruptions and information overload (Kumar et al., 2020). Privacy is another major concern, as sensitive academic discussions may be exposed to external parties.

Academic Communication Tools: Research into dedicated academic communication tools highlights the importance of secure, focused platforms. Platforms like Microsoft Teams and Google Classroom offer some degree of control but are often complex and not optimized for informal peer-to-peer communication (Singh & Sharma, 2021). These tools are primarily designed for structured learning management rather than dynamic, real-time messaging.

Need for College-Specific Solutions: Several studies emphasize the need for college-specific communication platforms that balance security, ease of use, and academic focus (Patel et al., 2019). Such platforms should restrict access to verified members, support group management, and provide features for announcements and departmental coordination.

Summary: The literature suggests that while existing tools offer basic communication capabilities, there is a gap in solutions tailored for academic institutions. BDC Messenger aims to fill this gap by providing a secure, distraction-free, and college-specific platform for students and faculty.

4. System Analysis

Requirements Gathering

BDC Messenger was designed after a thorough analysis of the communication needs of students and faculty at Late Babanrao Deshmukh College, Camp, Amravati. The requirements were gathered through interviews, surveys, and observation of existing communication practices.

Functional Requirements:
  • User registration and authentication
  • Real-time messaging (individual and group)
  • Group creation and admin controls
  • Profile management
  • Password reset and account recovery
  • Responsive/mobile-friendly interface
Non-Functional Requirements:
  • Data privacy and security
  • High availability and reliability
  • Scalability for future growth

Stakeholder Analysis

  • Students: Need a platform for academic discussions, peer communication, and group collaboration.
  • Faculty: Require tools for departmental coordination, announcements, and student engagement.
  • Administrators: Need control over user access, group management, and platform security.

Use Case Diagrams and Scenarios

  • Register/Login: Users create accounts and authenticate securely.
  • Send Message: Users exchange messages in real time.
  • Create Group: Admins create groups and manage members.
  • Update Profile: Users edit their personal information.
  • Receive Announcement: Faculty and admins broadcast important updates.
Sample Scenario: A faculty member creates a group for a specific course, adds students, and posts announcements. Students discuss assignments and ask questions in a distraction-free environment.

System Constraints

  • Only verified college members can access the platform.
  • All communication is logged for accountability.
  • The system must support concurrent users without performance degradation.

Summary

The system analysis phase ensured that BDC Messenger addresses the unique requirements of the college community, focusing on security, usability, and academic relevance.

5. System Design

Architecture Overview

BDC Messenger is built using a modern fullstack architecture, comprising a React-based frontend, a Node.js/Express backend, and MongoDB for data storage. Real-time communication is enabled via Socket.io.

Architecture Layers:
  • Frontend: React (Vite), Zustand for state management, Tailwind CSS/DaisyUI for UI.
  • Backend: Node.js, Express, Socket.io for real-time messaging, Mongoose for MongoDB interaction.
  • Database: MongoDB stores user profiles, messages, groups, and authentication data.

Data Flow Diagrams

User Registration/Login:
  1. User submits registration/login form.
  2. Backend validates credentials and issues JWT token.
  3. Frontend stores token and authenticates future requests.
Messaging:
  1. User sends a message via frontend.
  2. Message is transmitted to backend via Socket.io.
  3. Backend saves message to database and broadcasts to recipients.
Group Management:
  1. Admin creates a group and adds members.
  2. Backend updates group data and notifies members.

UI/UX Design Principles

  • Distraction-Free Layout: Minimalist design focused on academic communication.
  • Responsive Design: Adapts to mobile, tablet, and desktop screens.
  • Accessibility: High-contrast themes, readable fonts, and intuitive navigation.
  • User Roles: Admins have additional controls for group management.

Security and Privacy Considerations

  • Authentication: JWT-based login, secure password storage.
  • Authorization: Role-based access for group admin features.
  • Data Protection: All communication is encrypted in transit.
  • Privacy: Only verified college members can access the platform.

System Components

  • Authentication Module: Handles registration, login, password reset.
  • Chat Module: Real-time messaging, message deletion, group chats.
  • Profile Module: User info, profile picture, and settings.
  • Admin Module: Group creation, member management, announcements.

Summary

The system design ensures BDC Messenger is robust, secure, and tailored for academic use, with a scalable architecture ready for future enhancements.

6. Implementation

Technology Stack

  • Frontend: React (Vite), Zustand, DaisyUI, Tailwind CSS
  • Backend: Node.js, Express, Socket.io, Mongoose
  • Database: MongoDB
  • Other: JWT for authentication, Cloudinary for profile images

Key Modules

Authentication:
  • User registration and login with email/password
  • Secure password hashing and JWT token issuance
  • Password reset flow with email verification
Chat and Messaging:
  • Real-time messaging using Socket.io
  • Support for individual and group chats
  • Message deletion and instant UI updates
Group Management:
  • Group creation by admins
  • Add/remove members, update group name/photo
  • Admin controls for group settings
Profile and Settings:
  • Editable user profile (name, email, about, profile picture)
  • Settings for account, privacy, personalization, and security

Deployment Steps

  1. Install dependencies in both frontend and backend folders using npm install.
  2. Start backend server with npm run dev in the backend folder.
  3. Start frontend server with npm run dev in the frontend folder.
  4. Access the app at http://localhost:5174 (or the shown port).

Challenges and Solutions

  • Real-time Communication: Implemented Socket.io for instant messaging.
  • Mobile Responsiveness: Used Tailwind CSS and DaisyUI for adaptive layouts.
  • Security: Applied JWT authentication and role-based access for group admin features.
  • User Experience: Designed a distraction-free, college-specific interface.

Summary

The implementation phase resulted in a robust, user-friendly chat application tailored for academic use, with all major features working seamlessly across devices.

7. Testing and Evaluation

Testing Strategy

BDC Messenger underwent rigorous testing to ensure reliability, security, and usability. The testing process included unit testing, integration testing, user acceptance testing, and performance evaluation.

Test Cases

  • Authentication: Register with valid/invalid credentials, login/logout flow, password reset and email verification
  • Messaging: Send/receive messages in real time, message deletion and UI update, group chat functionality
  • Group Management: Create group, add/remove members, update group name and photo, admin controls and permissions
  • Profile and Settings: Edit profile information, change theme and personalization settings, privacy and security toggles

User Acceptance Testing

Pilot users (students and faculty) tested the application and provided feedback on usability, responsiveness, and feature completeness. Suggestions were incorporated to improve navigation, mobile experience, and group management.

Performance and Security Testing

  • Load testing for concurrent users
  • Validation of JWT authentication and secure data transmission
  • Database integrity checks

Results

  • All major features functioned as intended across devices.
  • Real-time messaging and group management were robust and reliable.
  • The app was found to be distraction-free and suitable for academic use.
  • Security and privacy requirements were met.

Summary

Testing and evaluation confirmed that BDC Messenger is a stable, secure, and user-friendly platform for college communication.

8. Results and Discussion

Achievements

BDC Messenger successfully delivered a secure, distraction-free communication platform tailored for the academic needs of Late Babanrao Deshmukh College, Camp, Amravati. The application met all functional and non-functional requirements, including real-time messaging, group management, user authentication, and mobile responsiveness.

User Feedback

Pilot users reported that the platform was easy to use, visually appealing, and effective for academic discussions and departmental coordination. The group chat and admin controls were especially appreciated for organizing study groups and sharing announcements.

Comparison with Public Messaging Apps

Unlike public platforms, BDC Messenger restricted access to verified college members, minimizing distractions and privacy concerns. Academic discussions were more focused, and departmental communication was streamlined.

Limitations

  • The initial version does not include voice/video calling or file sharing.
  • Push notifications and end-to-end encryption are planned for future releases.
  • Integration with college systems (attendance, announcements) is yet to be implemented.

Discussion

The project demonstrates the value of a dedicated communication platform for academic institutions. By addressing privacy, usability, and control, BDC Messenger provides a foundation for further enhancements and broader adoption within the college.

Summary

BDC Messenger achieved its primary aim and objectives, offering a robust solution for college-specific communication. The results validate the need for such platforms in educational settings.

9. Future Scope

BDC Messenger lays a strong foundation for secure, academic-focused communication within the college. The following enhancements are proposed for future development:

  • Voice and Video Calling: Integrate real-time audio and video calls for richer collaboration.
  • Push Notifications: Enable instant alerts for new messages, announcements, and group activities on mobile devices.
  • End-to-End Encryption: Implement advanced security to protect user conversations and data privacy.
  • File Sharing: Allow users to share documents, images, and other media within chats and groups.
  • Admin Dashboard: Develop a centralized dashboard for administrators to manage users, groups, and platform settings.
  • Integration with College Systems: Connect with attendance, timetable, and announcement modules for seamless academic management.
  • Progressive Web App (PWA) Support: Make the app installable and usable offline for better accessibility.
  • Multi-language Support: Add localization features to cater to a diverse user base.
  • AI-Powered Features: Introduce smart search, chatbots, and automated moderation for improved user experience.
These future enhancements will further strengthen BDC Messenger’s role as a comprehensive communication platform for the college, supporting evolving academic and administrative needs.
